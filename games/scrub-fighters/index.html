Scrub Fighters

ScrubFighters is still a work in progress

“In the words of TLC “A scrub is a guy that thinks he’s fly” and thats just what these fighters think they are. So take up arms against the other scrubs and become the one and only Scrub lord, in this one and only scrub free for all..”

Scrub Fighters is the worlds simplest fighting game for people who dont know how to play fighting games. Its fast paced button mashing fun.

The idea is that the player would have 4 simple controlls: Attack up and down and Defend up and Down. With these four moves the goal for each player is to make the enemy’s health reach 0 before theirs does.

The game has gone through two major stages so far, the initial prototype and the overhaul.

Initial Prototype

The first version of Scrub Fighters had a very simple concept of two characters punching each other until one of them has 0 health.

The Initial mock up of the game was just 3 boxes for each player, a box for the head hitbox, one for the body hitbox, and one for the fist. The player would use their fist to either block or attack the enemy. This meant that a player can only pick one move at a time, however a side effect of using the players fist to both block and attack is that while attacking up, the player would essentially also be blocking up.

This version focused primarily on a single player experience, and as such contained a tower climb that would see the player fight a new opponent in each battle (This was half implamented, with the player being able to go onto the next battle but characters appearing the same as i had not focused on art at the time).

After various animation tests i realised that i was unsatisfied with the direction which the game was going, and as such, I started making the game again with the things i learnt from this prototype.

Scrub Fighters Overhaul

With this version of the game I decided to start of by making a enjoyable animation, one that would feel smooth and fun to play. To make this animation feel smoother, I gave the character a wider range of animations.

Like previously, players would have an animation for each of the four moves, attacking and defending both up and down. However now they would also crouch down, and have a seperate animation for getting hit.

Furthermore I decided to change the way in which the player attacks, with this version the player now shoots an object instead of throwing their hand, this helped make the game feel faster and more fluid, and means that I would be able to give characters unique attacks (shot types) and animations.

Moreover, in this version my focus was on the multiplayer. I did this for a few reasons, primarily because it would mean I can concentrate on making the game feel fluid for multiple people to play, but also because I felt more of the multiplayer code could be used for singleplayer than visa versa.
